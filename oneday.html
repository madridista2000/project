Mtskheta – Tbilisi Tour

Visit the modern and old capitals of Georgia on a one-day tour to Tbilisi and Mtskheta with an exciting route. Both cities are wealthy in points of interest and noteworthy destinations. Feel the beauty of a cozy, beautiful and sentimental Tbilisi, at that point cast your mind back into the medieval view of Mtskheta, the foremost prevalent touristic city of Georgia. We begin our trip at 09:00. Our direct picks you up from hotel and takes you to our company head-office to check-in for the visit. We begin at Holy Trinity cathedral one of the biggest conventional cathedrals in the world. At that point we go to the Metekhi Church and the monument of Vakhtang Gorgasali, the legendary founder of Tbilisi. Take a cable car to Narikala Fortress to enjoy a breathtaking panoramic see of the city. Walk down to the area of Sulfur bath houses passing by Tbilisi’s mosque. Walk through the Sharden area full of narrow roads with cozy cafés, wine tasting bars, souvenir shops, and breathe within the living past.

Tour to Kazbegi

Kazbegi and its surroundings are one of the must-see places in Georgia. Day trip gives you the opportunity to visit historic destinations, to enjoy some spectacular panoramic landscapes of the region and also you can taste well-known local food. The trip to Kazbegi starts from Tbilisi and heads to the north. This is the mainroad running for 220 km from Tbilisi to Vladikavkaz in Russia, a spectacular road with its stunning mountains. This road is important as it is the only legally available link connecting to Russia through the Caucasus Mountains.

Jinvali Water Dam and Ananuri Fortress

More info

The first sight to visit on the road is Jinvali water reservoir, which is a hydroelectric dam on the Aragvi River in Zhinvali. You stop along the way to snap photos of the wonderful scenery of blue coloured water and panoramic views. Then you explore Ananuri complex, which is close to Jinvali water dam and about 70 km away from Tbilisi. The fortress dates to XVI-XVIII centuries and it is situated to the foreland by the river Aragvi.
